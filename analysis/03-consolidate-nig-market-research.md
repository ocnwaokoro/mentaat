## This Document's UNIQUE Data (not duplicated elsewhere)

### Infrastructure Detail
- **Power:** Nigeria has **20 watts per capita** vs South Africa 767, Ghana 152
- Generator market: **$547.8M (2023)**
- Electricity tariffs surged **200%+ for Band A industrial customers**
- Chemical/Pharma sector alt energy spending **DOUBLED** in one year
- Textiles alt energy spending **4X increase**

### Connectivity
- Only **0.1% have fixed broadband**; ranked 129th globally
- **$4.89/month per Mbps** (10x US cost)
- Telco OPEX: N5.85T ($3.6B), 85% YoY increase
- Some states charge **N5K-N9K per meter of fiber** (right-of-way fees)
- 2G still **42% of connections**; 5G only 2.4%

### Port/Logistics Granularity
- **53 illegal checkpoints** between Mile 2 and Seme border (N50-100K per trip)
- Port congestion: **$55M daily**, N600B/month
- **5,000+ containers stuck** due to B'Odogwu platform rollout, N2T demurrage exposure
- **Maritime spending: $7-9B annually** to foreign ship owners
- Warehouse capacity: ~1 sq meter per capita vs 3+ in South Africa

### FX Crisis Detail
- GTBank slashed dollar limits: $1,500 → $500 → **$100**
- Letter of Credit payments dropped **57% YoY** ($912M → $391M)
- **$7B backlog** of FX demand by 2023; CBN unmet forwards: $2.4B
- 12 listed firms: **N1.4T aggregate FX losses**

### Brain Drain Numbers
- **$11B annual loss** from digital skills gaps
- **3.68M Nigerians emigrated** in 2 years
- **69% of 18-35 year olds** willing to relocate
- University of Lagos lost **60% of staff**
- Faculty-student ratios: **1:60** (ideal: 1:20), some depts **1:200**
- Doctor-patient ratio: **1:9,083** (WHO: 1:6,000)
- Salary gap: international N10-20M/yr vs Nigeria N3-5M (3-7x)
