## What This Document Is

A 10-tier catalog of every meaningful data source for researching Nigerian B2B opportunities. It's a meta-document — doesn't contain market data itself, but maps WHERE to find every type of primary and secondary data.

## The 10 Source Tiers

### Tier 1-3: Hard Data
- **World Bank Enterprise Surveys** — 15,000+ businesses, 2023 data, downloadable raw data
- **NBS (National Bureau of Statistics)** — quarterly business surveys, "Business Expectations Survey"
- **CBN** — MSME Development Fund reports, intervention programme data
- **MAN** — ~N50K associate membership gets full reports + member directory (= customer list)
- **Big 4 reports** — PwC MSME Survey, KPMG quarterly, EY tax guides, Deloitte PE surveys

### Tier 4-6: Industry Intelligence
- **NGX listed company annual reports** — notes to financials = quantified operational pain (diesel, FX losses)
- **Trade associations** — MAN, LCCI, NACCIMA, NASSI (100K+ SMEs), Shippers' Council, Freight Forwarders
- **Think tanks** — Tony Elumelu Foundation (10K+ entrepreneur surveys), Brookings, Harvard Africa Center

### Tier 7-8: Competitive Intel
- **Job boards** (Jobberman, LinkedIn) — job descriptions reveal product roadmaps
- **Social media** — Facebook Groups ("Nigeria Business Network", "Importers Association"), Twitter/X, WhatsApp groups
- **Stears Business** (~$10/month) — best data-driven Nigerian business analysis

### Tier 9-10: Unconventional
- **Procurement portals** — government buying patterns reveal service gaps
- **Court databases** — commercial dispute patterns = pain points
- **Physical markets** — Alaba International (electronics), Idumota (textiles), Computer Village (IT)
- **Direct observation** — "Shadow a business for a day" methodology

## Key Opportunities Implied
1. **Compliance-as-a-Service** — SON certification, NAFDAC registration flagged explicitly
2. **Logistics software** — multiple port/shipping data sources validate the pain
3. **Digitization of market traders** — "What software do they use? (Probably none)"
4. **Export enablement** — NEPC has exporters lists (= customer lists)
5. **Insurance/risk products** — NAICOM claims data reveals underserved segments

## Actionable Takeaways
- **Stears Business subscription ($10/month)** is the single best remote research tool
- **MAN associate membership (~N50K/$33)** gets full reports + customer directory
- **3-4 hour sprint** can yield 5-10 validated problems, 10-15 proven models, 20+ quotes
- **NGX annual reports** are an underused goldmine for quantified operational pain points
