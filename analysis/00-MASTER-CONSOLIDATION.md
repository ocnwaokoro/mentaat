## THE CORE THESIS

Nigerian businesses spend **N67-104T ($44-68B) annually** on workarounds to broken infrastructure, financial friction, and regulatory complexity. The opportunity is **capturing existing spend** with better solutions — not creating new demand.

## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORKS

### The NYC Constraint Filter
Most top-10 opportunities require physical presence. Only PURE SOFTWARE plays work remotely:
- B2B payments/treasury management
- HR/payroll compliance SaaS
- Invoice automation/matching
- Tax compliance automation
- FX exposure tools
- Cybersecurity (managed services)

### The "Visibility Layer" Thesis
The winning pattern: make invisible costs visible FIRST, then layer optimization and financing.
- Power costs → solar ROI justification → installer referral fees
- Invoice chaos → matching tool → factoring/early payment
- Tax deadlines → compliance calendar → accounting services referral

### The SaaS → Fintech Wedge
1. Payroll SaaS → salary advances + employee insurance
2. Invoice automation → invoice factoring + supply chain finance
3. Freight visibility → cargo insurance + driver financing
4. Clinic management → healthcare facility lending

### The "Sell Before Building" Mandate
Pattern to break: Research → Build → Launch → Nobody buys
New pattern: Pick → Call → Sell → Build only what's sold
