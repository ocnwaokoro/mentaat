## Core Research Findings

### The Workaround Economy
- **N104T ($67.8B) annually** spent on workarounds (25-30% of GDP)
- Conservative: N38.5T ($25.1B) | Mid-range: N67-75T ($43.6-48.9B)

### Top 5 Mega-Markets
1. Alternative Power: N22T ($14.3B) — grid collapsed 12x in 2024
2. SME Credit: N49.4T ($32.2B) gap — <5% MSMEs access credit, 35.5% rates
3. Logistics/3PL: N17T ($11.1B) — 21-day port dwell, transport = 30-50% of goods value
4. B2B Payments: N1.07 quadrillion volume — N75B failed payments growing 42%/yr
5. Post-Harvest Agriculture: N3.5T ($2.3B) losses — 50% of food lost

### CBN Survey Insights (1,900 businesses, Feb-Nov 2025)
- Top constraints: Insecurity (70.1%), Taxes (69.7%), Power (69.3%)
- Capacity utilization crashed 62% → 50.1% in one month (Oct → Nov)
- Credit Access Index turned positive in Aug for first time (-8.8 → +9.2)
- South-East consistently lowest confidence (18.7); North-East highest (52.7)

### MAN Quarterly Data
- Capacity crashed 57.66% → 47.72% (Q2 → Q4)
- Energy costs surged 145% (N275.6B → N676.6B)
- All top 5 constraints above 91% in Q4
- Naira depreciated 25.3% Q1 → Q4
