# PRD Review Checklist

Use this to validate any PRD against the disruptive innovation framework. Go section by section. Flag gaps, not just failures — a gap means "needs more thinking," not "kill the idea."

---

## Section 1: Target Segment

- [ ] Does the PRD clearly identify WHO is currently non-consuming or underserved?
- [ ] Is the target segment specific enough to act on? (Not just "Nigerians" — which Nigerians, where, earning what, doing what today?)
- [ ] Does the PRD explain what workaround or alternative the target currently uses (including "nothing")?
- [ ] Is there evidence that this segment would *pull* this product into their lives? (vs. needing to be convinced)

**If the target is mainstream customers already served by incumbents, this is not disruption — flag it.**

---

## Section 2: Product Positioning

- [ ] Is the product deliberately simpler/cheaper than existing solutions?
- [ ] Does the PRD acknowledge what the product sacrifices compared to premium alternatives?
- [ ] Is "good enough" clearly defined — what minimum bar does the product meet?
- [ ] Is the product sampleable or testable at low risk to the consumer?

**If the product aims to be the best on the market from day one, flag as sustaining innovation.**

---

## Section 3: Market Foothold

- [ ] Does the PRD identify whether entry is low-end, new-market, or both?
- [ ] For low-end: what existing market is being entered at the bottom of?
- [ ] For new-market: what category is being created? Who are the first-time buyers?
- [ ] Is there a clear reason incumbents would ignore this segment (unattractive margins, too small, too hard to reach)?

---

## Section 4: Value Chain

- [ ] Does the PRD address how the product gets from production to customer?
- [ ] Are infrastructure gaps identified? (Power, logistics, cold chain, workforce)
- [ ] Is there a plan for building missing infrastructure, or does the PRD assume it exists?
- [ ] Does the value chain create assets that could generate independent revenue?
- [ ] Are waste/byproducts considered? Could they become secondary products?

**If the PRD only describes the product and not the system around it, it's incomplete. Flag it.**

See: [../framework/value-chain-design.md](../framework/value-chain-design.md)

---

## Section 5: Scale Strategy

- [ ] Does the PRD prioritize distribution breadth over feature depth?
- [ ] Is there a clear Phase 1 (minimum viable product + maximum distribution)?
- [ ] Is the feature roadmap sequenced AFTER achieving meaningful reach?
- [ ] Is the path from "good enough" to "better than incumbents" mapped?

**If the roadmap is feature-heavy before distribution is solved, flag as premature optimization.**

---

## Section 6: Cost Structure

- [ ] Are unit economics viable at the target customer's price point?
- [ ] Is the cost model fundamentally different from incumbents (not just "cheaper")?
- [ ] Does the PRD identify the volume needed for profitability?
- [ ] Are raw material costs addressed? (Waste-as-input? Local sourcing?)
- [ ] Is there dependency on subsidies or grants? If so, is there a path to self-sustaining economics?

**If profitability requires premium pricing, the cost structure doesn't match a disruptive strategy. Flag it.**

See: [../framework/cost-structure-patterns.md](../framework/cost-structure-patterns.md)

---

## Section 7: Growth Model

- [ ] Does growth come from converting non-consumers, or from competing with incumbents?
- [ ] Does the PRD project job creation, supply chain development, or economic multiplier effects?
- [ ] Are there network effects or compounding advantages as the product scales?

**If growth is purely market share theft, this is competitive innovation, not disruptive. May still be valid, but name it correctly.**

---

## Section 8: Maturation Path

- [ ] Can the product improve iteratively over time?
- [ ] Is there a credible path to meeting or exceeding mainstream quality?
- [ ] Can the product eventually move upmarket?
- [ ] Are there regulatory or policy trends that will favor this product over time?

---

## Section 9: Nigeria-Specific Checks

- [ ] Does the PRD account for infrastructure realities? (See [../nigeria-context/infrastructure-realities.md](../nigeria-context/infrastructure-realities.md))
- [ ] Does it avoid MNC failure patterns? (See [../nigeria-context/mnc-failure-patterns.md](../nigeria-context/mnc-failure-patterns.md))
- [ ] Is there a government engagement strategy? (See [../nigeria-context/government-leverage.md](../nigeria-context/government-leverage.md))
- [ ] Does the product respect existing consumer preferences and behavior?
- [ ] Is the workforce strategy local-first?
- [ ] Is the business model resilient to Naira volatility / import dependency?

---

## Summary Template

After reviewing, summarize:

**Disruption Score:** X/8 criteria met (strong fit)
**Critical Gaps:** [list criteria with no fit or unknown]
**Nigeria Readiness:** [high/medium/low] with specific concerns
**Recommendation:** [proceed / revise specific sections / rethink fundamentally]
