# Government Leverage

How to work with Nigerian government structures to strengthen your disruptive innovation.

---

## Core Principle

Government is not an obstacle to route around — it's a lever to pull. But you earn the right to pull it by creating undeniable local value first.

---

## The Tolaram Playbook

**Sequence matters:**

1. **Invest first, ask later.** Build factories, create jobs, pay taxes, train workers. Do this BEFORE seeking policy favors.
2. **Create dependency.** When your operations employ thousands, generate tax revenue, and build infrastructure the government didn't, you become strategically important.
3. **Lobby from strength.** Tolaram's import ban on instant noodles was only possible because they had already demonstrated massive local manufacturing capacity.
4. **Align your ask with national interest.** "Ban imports" sounds protectionist. "Protect local jobs, ensure food safety, keep manufacturing domestic" is a national interest argument.

---

## Policy Levers to Target

### Import Restrictions
- If you manufacture locally, import bans on competing products lock out foreign competition
- Requires demonstrating that local production meets quality standards
- Strongest when paired with food safety or public health arguments

### Tax Incentives
- Free trade zones offer tax exemptions, duty-free imports of raw materials, profit remittance
- Lagos Free Zone model: Tolaram built the zone, government provided the fiscal framework
- Target incentives that reduce your cost structure (import duty exemptions on equipment/inputs)

### Quality Standards
- Lobby for quality regulations that your product already meets
- This creates a barrier for cheaper imports that don't meet the standard
- The 2023 toxic noodle incident (Taiwan/Malaysia) validated Nigeria's local quality control

### Subsidies
- Especially relevant for products with environmental or energy-access benefits
- Charcoal briquettes could qualify for energy transition subsidies
- Align with government's stated development goals

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Asking for favors before demonstrating value** — governments respond to leverage, not pitches
- **Treating corruption as an excuse to disengage** — Tolaram didn't wait for a perfect system; they built value within the existing one
- **Ignoring state/local government** — Nigeria is federal. State-level relationships matter for manufacturing permits, land, and local operations
- **Assuming policy is permanent** — political cycles change. Build a business that works WITH policy support but doesn't collapse without it

---

## Framing Your Government Ask

| What You Want | How to Frame It |
|---|---|
| Import ban | "Protect local jobs and food safety" |
| Tax exemption | "Enable investment that creates X thousand jobs" |
| Land/permits | "Build infrastructure that serves the entire region" |
| Quality regulation | "Ensure consumer safety with standards our product already meets" |
| Subsidies | "Accelerate energy access / food security for underserved populations" |
