# Nigeria Market Landscape

Key characteristics of the Nigerian market relevant to disruptive innovation strategy.

---

## Consumer Segments

### The Base of the Pyramid (Primary Target for Disruption)
- 200M+ population, majority low-income
- High price sensitivity — products must work at extremely low price points
- Strong existing purchasing behavior for daily necessities (food, fuel, transport, communication)
- Will actively seek and pay for things they value (phones, motorcycles, certain foods)
- Will NOT adopt things pushed on them that don't match their priorities

### The "Ephemeral Middle Class"
- The segment MNCs typically target — and overestimate
- Exists in urban centers but is smaller and less stable than projections suggest
- MNCs who build business models around this segment frequently fail and exit

### Urban vs. Rural
- Urban: more infrastructure, more competition, more exposure to global products
- Rural: more non-consumption, more infrastructure gaps, more opportunity for disruption
- Distribution to rural areas is the primary logistics challenge

---

## Non-consumption Zones (Where Disruption Lives)

Look for categories where people either have nothing or are using terrible workarounds:

- **Energy/cooking fuel** — firewood, kerosene, raw biomass. Expensive relative to income, inefficient, unhealthy
- **Financial services** — large unbanked population, informal savings/lending systems
- **Cold chain/food preservation** — post-harvest losses are massive due to lack of refrigeration
- **Water** — access to clean water remains inconsistent outside urban centers
- **Healthcare** — especially diagnostics and basic care in rural areas
- **Education/training** — demand for skills exceeds institutional capacity

---

## What People Pull Into Their Lives

These categories reveal genuine demand — people spend money on these despite poverty:
- Mobile phones and airtime
- Motorcycles (okada)
- Packaged/convenient food (Indomie being the prime example)
- Generator fuel (diesel/petrol for personal power generation)
- Fashion and personal care

**The pattern:** Convenience, status, and immediate utility drive purchasing. Products that require behavior change without clear immediate benefit struggle.

---

## Staple Foods Context

- Rice, cassava, yam, and corn are primary staples
- Instant noodles (Indomie) have joined this tier — remarkable for a foreign product
- Food innovation must respect existing taste preferences (Indomie added spicy Nigerian variants)
- Sampleability matters — if someone can try it cheaply and immediately see value, adoption accelerates

---

## Key Market Dynamics

- **Informal economy dominates** — most retail happens through informal channels, market stalls, street vendors
- **Cash-heavy** — though mobile money is growing
- **Trust is local** — people trust products they see their neighbors using
- **Import dependence** — many consumer goods are imported, creating opportunities for local manufacturing arbitrage
- **Currency volatility** — Naira fluctuations make import-dependent businesses fragile
