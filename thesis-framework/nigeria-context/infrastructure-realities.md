# Infrastructure Realities in Nigeria

What exists, what doesn't, and what that means for your innovation strategy.

---

## What You Cannot Assume Exists

### Reliable Power
- Grid power is inconsistent across the country
- Most businesses and many households rely on generators
- Any production facility needs its own power solution
- This is a cost you MUST budget for — not an edge case

### Logistics Networks
- National logistics infrastructure is underdeveloped
- Road quality varies dramatically by region
- No reliable third-party logistics at scale for most product categories
- If your product needs to reach rural areas, you likely need your own fleet or exclusive partnerships

### Cold Chain
- Virtually nonexistent outside major urban centers
- Massive post-harvest food losses as a direct result
- Any product requiring temperature control needs its own cold chain infrastructure

### Skilled Workforce (in Your Specific Domain)
- Nigeria has talented, educated people — but specialized skills in your niche may not exist
- Don't interpret this as "no talent" — it means you need to train
- In-house training programs are an investment, not a cost

### Waste Management
- Industrial waste disposal is poorly regulated and poorly executed
- This creates both a problem (your waste) and an opportunity (others' waste as your input)

---

## What DOES Exist

### Massive Consumer Market
- 200M+ people who need things and will pay for things they value
- This is the fundamental asset

### Entrepreneurial Energy
- Informal economy is vast and sophisticated
- People are resourceful problem-solvers by necessity
- Distribution through informal retail channels can reach anywhere

### Mobile Connectivity
- Phone penetration is high and growing
- Mobile money adoption is increasing
- Digital coordination of supply chains is feasible

### Port Infrastructure (Emerging)
- Lagos Free Zone + Lekki Deep Sea Port (Tolaram-led)
- Improves import/export logistics for Lagos region
- Signals government openness to private infrastructure investment

### Agricultural Base
- Massive agricultural output (palm oil, cassava, rice, etc.)
- Generates abundant biomass waste
- Existing farmer networks for sourcing

---

## Strategic Implications

1. **Budget for infrastructure as a core cost, not an overhead surprise**
2. **Infrastructure you build can become its own revenue stream** (Tolaram logistics model)
3. **Partner with or build on existing informal networks** rather than trying to formalize everything
4. **Train your workforce and view it as building your moat** — competitors can't easily replicate a trained team
5. **Every infrastructure gap is also a business opportunity** — power, logistics, cold chain, waste processing
