# Case Study: Palm Waste Charcoal Briquettes (PWCB)

Framework application example — how the 8 criteria were used to discover and validate a novel disruptive innovation.

---

## The Discovery Process

### Starting Point: Choose an Overlooked Segment First
- Rural communities in West Africa
- People who already exhibit purchasing behavior for daily necessities
- Narrowed to food/cooking because it's universal and the product is sampleable

### Finding the Problem
- 950M people in Africa rely on inefficient stoves and solid fuels
- People earning $40-100/month spend ~1/3 of income on cooking fuel (mainly firewood)
- Fuel burns inefficiently → buy more → smoke inhalation → health consequences
- Existing solutions (Mukuru Clean Stoves) focus on the stove, not the fuel — misplaced emphasis

### Finding the Raw Material
- Palm oil production generates 90% waste (only 10% becomes oil)
- Palm kernel shells (PKS) are abundant, growing, and largely unmanaged
- Already used informally as raw fuel — proves combustibility and market acceptance
- Henry Ford precedent: sawmill waste → charcoal briquettes (1919) — same pattern

---

## 8 Criteria Applied

| # | Criterion | PWCB Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Overlooked segment | Rural/low-income households burning firewood and raw biomass |
| 2 | "Worse" but good enough | Requires behavioral adaptation, different combustion — but cheaper and sufficient heat |
| 3 | Foothold | Low-end (cheaper than firewood/charcoal) + new-market (no briquette industry exists in Nigeria) |
| 4 | Value chain reimagined | Transforms palm oil waste disposal problem into fuel production input |
| 5 | Scale core solution | Efficient briquette production first, premium/industrial applications later |
| 6 | Different cost structure | Raw material is waste (near-zero input cost). Volume + affordability model |
| 7 | Non-consumption → growth | New fuel source for communities without commercial fuel access |
| 8 | Can mature | Align with sustainability regulations, carbon credits, quality improvements over time |

## Key Barriers Identified

### Supply-Side
- Palm oil industry is fragmented (large plantations + smallholders with different priorities)
- Supply is seasonal and unpredictable
- Waste procurement has become monetized — mills no longer give it away free
- Price volatility tied to harvest yields
- High humidity → moisture content issues → reduced fuel efficiency

### Demand-Side
- Must position as distinct fuel category, NOT a charcoal substitute
- Environmental appeal alone won't drive adoption — needs to solve the cooking problem better/cheaper
- Consumer skepticism toward unfamiliar products

### Logistics
- Transport from palm oil mills to processing facilities
- Collection, preprocessing, storage optimization all underdeveloped
- No unified large-scale approach exists

---

## Strategic Lessons (from East African briquette research)

1. **Position as own category** — not "alternative charcoal" but a new type of fuel
2. **Stimulate demand via policy** — government incentives, expanded applications beyond cooking
3. **Market-driven, not mission-driven** — lead with cost/convenience, not environmental benefit
4. **Target industrial first** — industrial markets, high-end barbecue, transitioning firewood users as entry points
5. **Tap green funding for R&D** — Earthshot Prize, carbon credits, sustainability funds for development capital
6. **Learn from Asian briquette industries** — cost-effective production models exist in Indonesia/Malaysia
7. **Lobby for regulatory support** — tax incentives, subsidies to compete with established charcoal
8. **Industry unity** — collective body for policy advocacy, quality standards, resource pooling

## Why This Example Matters

This isn't just a product proposal — it's a demonstration of the framework in action:
- Started with segment (overlooked rural communities), not product
- Used the pull test (people already buy solid fuel — they'd switch for cheaper/better)
- Found raw material that's waste (near-zero input cost)
- Identified value chain transformation (waste disposal → fuel production)
- Mapped barriers honestly instead of hand-waving them

Use this as a template when running your own ideas through the framework.
