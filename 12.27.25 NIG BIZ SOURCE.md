## TIER 1: GOVERNMENT & INSTITUTIONAL DATA

### **Nigerian Government Sources:**

1. **National Bureau of Statistics (NBS)** ([nigerianstat.gov.ng](https://nigerianstat.gov.ng))  
     
   - Quarterly business surveys  
   - Sector-specific reports (manufacturing, agriculture, trade)  
   - **Gold:** "Nigerian Business Expectations Survey" \- shows what businesses are struggling with  
   - Free downloads, updated quarterly

   

2. **Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)** ([cbn.gov.ng](https://cbn.gov.ng))  
     
   - Economic reports section  
   - MSME Development Fund reports (shows where they're deploying capital)  
   - **Gold:** Annual reports from intervention programmes show validated problems

   

3. **Nigerian Export Promotion Council** ([nepc.gov.ng](https://nepc.gov.ng))  
     
   - Export challenges reports  
   - Lists of registered exporters (potential customers)  
   - Trade statistics by sector

   

4. **Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC)**  
     
   - Business registration data (shows what sectors are growing)  
   - Can search newly registered companies by sector

### **World Bank/IFC Deep Dives:**

1. **Enterprise Surveys** ([enterprisesurveys.org](https://enterprisesurveys.org))  
     
   - Nigeria-specific survey data (15,000+ businesses surveyed)  
   - **CRITICAL:** Download raw data, filter by sector, see exact pain points  
   - Shows: % citing electricity as problem, % citing access to finance, etc.  
   - Last Nigeria survey: 2023 (recent data)

   

2. **Doing Business Archives** ([doingbusiness.org](https://doingbusiness.org))  
     
   - Though discontinued, archives show specific friction points  
   - Time/cost to start business, get permits, trade across borders

   

3. **IFC Case Studies** ([ifc.org/country/nigeria](https://ifc.org/country/nigeria))  
     
   - Where IFC is investing \= validated opportunities  
   - Advisory projects reveal market gaps

## TIER 2: INDUSTRY ASSOCIATIONS (Primary Source Gold)

### **Nigerian Trade Associations:**

1. **Manufacturers Association of Nigeria (MAN)** ([manufacturersnigeria.org](https://manufacturersnigeria.org))  
     
   - Annual economic reviews (you cited this already)  
   - Sector-specific committees publish reports  
   - **Access:** Join as associate member (\~₦50K) for full report access  
   - Member directory \= direct customer list

   

2. **Lagos Chamber of Commerce (LCCI)** ([lagoschamber.com](https://lagoschamber.com))  
     
   - Quarterly economic reports  
   - SME surveys  
   - Events calendar (networking opportunities)

   

3. **Nigerian Association of Chambers of Commerce (NACCIMA)**  
     
   - State-by-state business reports  
   - Regional pain points differ \- useful for expansion planning

   

4. **National Association of Small Scale Industrialists (NASSI)**  
     
   - Represents 100,000+ SMEs  
   - Reports focused on SME-specific challenges

   

5. **Organized Private Sector (OPS) Reports**  
     
   - Coalition of business groups  
   - Policy advocacy documents \= list of problems they're lobbying to fix

### **Sector-Specific Associations:**

1. **Nigerian Shippers' Council** \- logistics/port issues  
2. **Freight Forwarders Association** \- customs/clearing pain points  
3. **Nigerian Medical Association** \- healthcare provider challenges  
4. **Association of Nigerian Electricity Distributors** \- power sector data  
5. **Nigerian Computer Society** \- IT adoption barriers

**Why these matter:**

- They survey members annually (primary data)  
- Advocacy documents list exact regulatory burdens  
- Member directories \= customer lists  
- Events \= direct access to decision-makers

## TIER 10: DIRECT OBSERVATION

### **Visit Physical Locations:**

1. **Alaba International Market** (Lagos) \- electronics/tech hub  
     
   - Talk to 20 traders, understand their operations  
   - What software do they use? (Probably none)

   

2. **Idumota Market** \- textiles/FMCG  
     
3. **Computer Village** \- IT/electronics  
     
4. **Industrial Estates:**  
     
   - Ikeja Industrial Estate  
   - Trans-Amadi (Port Harcourt)  
   - Kano Industrial Zone

**Method:**

- Spend 1 day walking around, observing  
- Count how many generators you see  
- Ask merchants: "What's your biggest expense?" "What takes most time?"

### **Shadow a Business for a Day:**

- Ask family to connect you with 1 factory manager  
- Spend 8 hours observing their workflow  
- Document every manual process, every complaint

## EFFICIENT SOURCING STRATEGY (3-4 Hours):

**Hour 1: Government/Institutional (Hard Data)**

- Download: NBS Business Survey, World Bank Enterprise Survey raw data, CBN MSME report  
- Extract: Top 3 problems cited by businesses

**Hour 2: Industry Associations (Validated Problems)**

- Read: MAN Economic Review, LCCI quarterly report  
- Note: Specific spending figures, member complaints

**Hour 3: Competitive Intel (What's Working)**

- Scan: Disrupt Africa \+ Techpoint for funded Nigerian B2B startups  
- Build list: 20 companies, their models

**Hour 4: Direct Voices (Qualitative)**

- Read: 10 BusinessDay CEO interviews  
- Listen: 3 podcast episodes with Nigerian founders  
- Extract: Direct quotes about pain points

**Output:**

- 5-10 validated problems (quantified with data)  
- 10-15 proven business models (from competition)  
- 20+ direct quotes from business leaders
