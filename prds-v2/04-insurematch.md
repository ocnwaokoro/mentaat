# PRD 04: InsureMatch — First-Time Insurance Enablement for Nigerian Businesses

## 1. Executive Summary

99.66% of Nigerians have no insurance. Not bad insurance. Not underinsurance. Nothing.

A welder in Aba with N15M in equipment, a food manufacturer in Ikeja with a N200M factory, a market trader in Onitsha with N8M in rotating stock — all operating with zero coverage against fire, theft, flood, liability, or business interruption. When a loss event strikes, the business ends. There is no recovery path, no safety net, no financial buffer. The owner starts over from zero, or does not start over at all.

This is not because insurance does not exist in Nigeria. Fifty-plus licensed insurers hold N3.9T in assets and grew premiums 56% year-over-year to N1.56T in 2024. The product exists. The problem is that insurance distribution is fundamentally broken: it flows through fragmented agents who each represent one or two insurers, who target only the largest corporates, who skip needs assessment entirely, and who disappear at claims time. The result is a system that actively excludes 99.66% of the market.

InsureMatch does not fix insurance distribution. It replaces it.

InsureMatch is a first-time insurance enablement platform. A business enters its profile — sector, assets, location, employees, existing coverage (almost always "none") — and receives a free, AI-generated risk assessment that quantifies their exposure in naira. "You have N85M in unprotected assets. A single fire would end your business. You are currently 0% insured." This risk assessment is the product. It is valuable on its own, before a single insurer partnership exists, because most Nigerian businesses have never had anyone map their risk exposure. The assessment creates a moment of pull — not "you should buy insurance" (push) but "you are sitting on N85M of unprotected risk and here is exactly how exposed you are" (pull). The business sees, for the first time, the gap between what they have and what they could lose.

When the business is ready to act, InsureMatch pulls quotes from partner insurers, presents a side-by-side comparison of coverage, exclusions, and price, and facilitates purchase — all in under 15 minutes. The business pays nothing. InsureMatch earns 15-25% commission from insurers on every policy sold, which is the standard industry commission structure. When a claim occurs, InsureMatch does not disappear. It advocates on behalf of the business — guiding document preparation, tracking claim status, and pushing back on insurer delays or unfair denials. This claims advocacy is the long-term differentiator that breaks the "insurance is a scam" narrative perpetuated by every denied claim and every vanished agent.

The platform attaches naturally to other products in the suite — generator insurance at point of asset registration in GenTrack, cargo insurance at point of shipment in ClearFast, trade credit insurance at point of invoice in PayMatch. This embedded insurance at point of need is the most disruptive distribution channel in the model, because it reaches the business at the exact moment the risk is real and the need is felt.

PolicyBazaar in India proved this model at scale, raising over $1B and reaching a $6B valuation in a market with 4% insurance penetration. Nigeria's penetration is 0.34% — twelve times lower — making the non-consumption opportunity twelve times larger. Over time, the risk profiling data generated by thousands of business assessments becomes an independent proprietary asset — a risk intelligence layer that no incumbent insurer possesses, because no one has ever systematically profiled Nigerian SME risk at scale.

MVP ships in 4-6 weeks, with insurer partnership onboarding as the critical path item. The risk assessment ships independently on day one — no insurer partnership required. First commission revenue follows the first policy sale.

## 2. Market Opportunity

**Nigeria's insurance market is defined by near-total non-consumption.**

This is not a market share fight. There is no market share to fight over. 99.66% of the addressable population has never purchased any form of insurance. The opportunity is not redistribution — it is creation.

### The Numbers

- **Insurance penetration:** 0.34% of the population has any form of insurance. Developed markets exceed 20%. India sits at 4%. Nigeria is not behind — it is at zero.
- **Gross premiums:** N1.56T in 2024, growing 56% year-over-year — the fastest growth rate of any financial services segment in Nigeria, outpacing payments (42% growth in failed payments alone), lending, and banking.
- **Total industry assets:** N3.9T with 46.1% growth — insurers are well-capitalized and actively seeking distribution channels to deploy this capital.
- **Regulatory tailwind:** NAICOM is actively pushing for increased penetration. The regulator wants more Nigerians insured, which means favorable treatment for distribution platforms that expand the market rather than redistribute existing customers.
- **Self-insurance trap:** Nigerian businesses spend billions annually on self-insurance workarounds — generators (N22T market) replace power insurance, boreholes replace water supply insurance, security guards replace theft/liability insurance. These workarounds cost 5-20x more than proper insurance coverage but feel safer because the business controls the spend.
- **The awareness gap:** Most Nigerian SMEs cannot name three types of business insurance beyond fire coverage. They do not know they need product liability insurance, key-man insurance, business interruption insurance, or professional indemnity. The first company to educate and convert simultaneously wins the market.
- **Proven global model:** PolicyBazaar (India) raised $1B+ and reached a $6B valuation. Compare the Market (UK), GoCompare (UK), and Policygenius (US) built substantial businesses on the same mechanic. Nigeria has zero equivalent platforms for B2B insurance comparison.
- **Commercial paper signal:** N1.58T in commercial paper issuances (107% YoY growth) shows Nigerian businesses are sophisticated enough to use financial instruments. They are not avoiding insurance out of financial illiteracy — they are avoiding it because the procurement experience is broken and no one has ever explained why they need it.

### The Wedge

No comprehensive B2B insurance comparison platform exists in Nigeria. Insurers sell through agents who represent one or two companies, not the market. Businesses cannot compare without calling 10 different agents — and most businesses never make the first call because they do not know what to ask for.

InsureMatch's wedge is not comparison. It is the risk assessment — the moment a business sees, quantified in naira, how exposed they are. The comparison engine is the delivery mechanism. The risk awareness is the product.

### Non-Consumption Framing

We are not competing for the 0.34% who are insured. We are enabling the 99.66% who have nothing. Every policy InsureMatch sells expands the total insurance market. Every business we reach is a first-time buyer. This is not market share redistribution — it is market creation.

This is the Indomie parallel: Tolaram did not compete for existing fast-food customers. They created 200 million noodle eaters who had never seen the product before. InsureMatch creates insured businesses that have never spoken to an insurance agent.

- **Current insurance status:** Zero. Has never purchased any form of insurance. Has never spoken to an insurance agent. Does not know what types of business insurance exist. If asked, would say "insurance is for big companies" or "insurance is a scam — they never pay." The concept of insuring a workshop against fire is foreign.
- **Risk exposure:** Entire livelihood is concentrated in physical assets that are completely unprotected. A single fire, flood, theft, or equipment failure eliminates everything. Has seen neighboring businesses destroyed by fire and watched the owner start over from nothing, or not start over at all.
- **Decision-making:** Owner makes all decisions personally. No finance team, no admin staff handling procurement. Decisions happen fast when the value is clear. Communicates primarily via WhatsApp and phone calls.
- **What pull looks like for this persona:** "You have N15M in equipment in this workshop. If there was a fire tonight, you would lose everything. For N180K per year — N15K per month — you could protect all of it. Here is exactly what is covered and what is not." The pull trigger is the naira-quantified gap between their assets and their coverage (zero).

**Why this persona matters for disruption:** This is the non-consumer that incumbent insurance agents will never serve. The premiums are too small (N100-500K per year), the locations too scattered, the education effort too high, and there is no existing relationship to leverage. ### Secondary Persona: The Underinsured Manufacturer (Partially Served)

## 4. Problem Deep Dive

### Why 99.66% Have Nothing

The problem is not that businesses reject insurance. It is that the system is designed in a way that makes it impossible for most businesses to access insurance. Here is the step-by-step breakdown:

**Step 0: No Entry Point (Where Most Businesses Stop)**

The vast majority of Nigerian SMEs never reach Step 1. They have never been approached by an insurance agent. They have never searched for insurance. They do not know what types of business insurance exist, how much it costs, or how to evaluate whether they need it. The entry point does not exist for them. Insurance is not rejected — it is invisible.

This is non-consumption in its purest form. The barrier is not price (many businesses could afford N100-500K per year in premiums). The barrier is that no one has ever explained, in terms a business owner understands, what insurance does, why they need it, and how to get it. The distribution system was never built to reach them.

**Step 1: Trigger Event (For the Few Who Get Here)**

A business realizes it might need insurance. This realization is almost always reactive — a neighboring factory burned down, a truck carrying N20M of goods was hijacked, a key employee died and the business nearly collapsed. Occasionally, a bank requires insurance as a loan condition. Proactive insurance shopping is extremely rare.

**Step 2: Finding an Agent**

The MD asks their network: "Do you know a good insurance person?" This produces a single agent, typically tied to one or two insurance companies. The agent is incentivized to sell the products of their principal insurer, not to find the best coverage for the client. There is no equivalent of "shopping around" because there is no easy way to compare across insurers.

**Step 3: Needs Assessment (Usually Skipped)**

A thorough insurance needs assessment would evaluate: all physical assets and their replacement value, revenue exposure to business interruption, liability exposure from products/services, key employee dependency, cargo/transit risks, professional indemnity needs, and regulatory compliance requirements. In practice, the agent asks "what do you want to insure?" and the MD says "the factory." The agent writes a fire policy. Everything else goes uncovered.

**Step 4: Quote and Purchase**

The agent provides a single quote from their insurer. The MD has no benchmark for whether the price is competitive, whether the coverage limits are adequate, or whether the exclusions contain hidden traps. Common problems:

- **Underinsurance:** Factory worth N500M insured for N100M. In the event of a claim, the insurer invokes the "average clause" and pays only 20% of the loss.
- **Exclusion traps:** Fire policy excludes fires caused by electrical faults — the #1 cause of factory fires in Nigeria. Generator-caused fires may be excluded entirely.
- **Sum insured stagnation:** Policy renewed annually at the same sum insured while naira depreciates 25-40% per year, making the coverage worth less every year.
- **Missing coverage types:** No business interruption coverage means the business gets a check for the physical damage but nothing for the months of lost revenue during reconstruction.

**Step 5: Claims (Where the Real Pain Lives)**

When a loss occurs, the business discovers their coverage is inadequate, excluded, or both. Claims processing takes 3-12 months. Documentation requirements are onerous and unfamiliar. The agent who sold the policy is nowhere to be found. The business feels cheated, reinforces the cultural narrative that "insurance is a scam," and tells every other business owner they know. This negative word-of-mouth is the single biggest barrier to insurance penetration growth. Every failed claim perpetuates non-consumption for hundreds of other businesses in the same community.

**Step 6: Business Impact of Being Uninsured**

- **Total loss events:** A factory fire with no insurance is a business-ending event. No recovery possible. 7.2M MSMEs shut down in 2023-2024 — how many were triggered by uninsured loss events?
- **Cargo losses:** Goods in transit worth N10-50M per shipment. One hijacking or accident without marine/cargo insurance wipes out months of profit.
- **Liability exposure:** Product defect, customer injury on premises, or environmental incident — without liability insurance, the business pays from operating capital.
- **Key-man risk:** If the founder/MD dies or is incapacitated, the business has no financial buffer to survive the transition.
- **Opportunity cost of self-insurance:** Money spent on generators (N22T market), boreholes, and security guards is money that could be deployed more productively if proper insurance transferred some of that risk. Businesses are already paying for risk mitigation — they are just paying 5-20x more than insurance would cost.

### The Push vs. Pull Tension

Insurance is inherently a push product. Businesses do not wake up wanting insurance. The natural trigger is a loss event — and by then it is too late. This is a genuine challenge for any insurance distribution business, and it must be addressed honestly rather than assumed away.

InsureMatch's answer is to design the risk assessment as a pull mechanism. The pull is not "buy insurance" — it is "understand your risk." The moment a business owner sees "You have N85M in unprotected assets and you are 0% insured," the abstract concept of insurance becomes a concrete, naira-denominated gap. This is the pull trigger. The business does not need to experience a loss event to feel urgency — they need to see, in their own numbers, how close they are to one.

This is structurally different from a cold insurance pitch. The risk assessment provides standalone value: a business owner who completes the assessment and never buys a policy still walks away knowing more about their risk exposure than they did before. This creates a genuine pull dynamic — the assessment is worth the 5-8 minutes it takes to complete, independent of any purchase decision.

The honest caveat: this pull mechanism will not convert every business immediately. Some will see the gap and still not act. But it converts non-consumption into awareness, and awareness into a pipeline. A business that knows it is 80% underinsured is fundamentally different from one that has never thought about insurance at all. The conversion may take weeks or months, but the awareness is permanent.

## 5. Solution Overview

InsureMatch is a web-based insurance enablement platform that guides Nigerian businesses from risk ignorance to risk coverage. The system operates in five stages:

### Stage 1: Business Risk Profiling

The business enters their profile through a guided wizard: sector/industry, number of employees, location(s), annual revenue range, physical assets (factory, warehouse, office, vehicles, equipment, inventory), nature of goods/services, existing insurance policies (if any). The wizard uses conditional logic — a manufacturer sees questions about raw materials and production equipment; a logistics company sees questions about fleet size and cargo values; a market trader sees questions about stock value and storage conditions. Profile completion takes 5-8 minutes.

For the downmarket persona (workshop owner, market trader), the wizard is simplified: fewer steps, slider-based asset estimation ("roughly how much is all your equipment worth?"), and plain-language descriptions instead of insurance jargon.

### Stage 2: Insurance Needs Assessment (AI-Powered) — The Core Product

Based on the business profile, InsureMatch generates a personalized risk assessment: which types of insurance the business needs (fire/property, burglary/theft, cargo/marine, public liability, product liability, business interruption, key-man/life, professional indemnity, money insurance, fidelity guarantee, machinery breakdown), what sum insured is appropriate for each type, and a risk priority score indicating which coverage to buy first given a limited budget.

The assessment includes an **insurance gap analysis** showing current coverage vs. recommended coverage. For most businesses, this will show 0% coverage against 100% recommended — the visual impact of seeing an empty bar against a full bar, quantified in naira, is the conversion mechanism.

**This assessment is the product.** It is valuable before any insurer partnership exists. It is the pull mechanism. It is what the business shares with partners and board members via WhatsApp. It is what creates the "you are 80% underinsured" moment that converts non-consumption into intent.

### Stage 3: Multi-Insurer Quote Engine

For each recommended insurance type, InsureMatch requests quotes from partner insurers via API or email-to-API bridge (for insurers without APIs, which is most of them initially). Quotes include: premium amount, coverage limits, key exclusions, deductible/excess amounts, claims process overview, insurer financial strength rating, and customer review scores (if available). The system normalizes these into a comparable format.

### Stage 4: Side-by-Side Comparison

Comparison tables show all quotes for each insurance type, ranked by InsureMatch's "value score" (a composite of price, coverage breadth, exclusion fairness, insurer reliability, and claims payment speed). Key exclusions are highlighted in red. Coverage gaps between policies are flagged. The interface makes it obvious which policy offers the best value, not just the lowest price.

### Stage 5: Purchase & Policy Management

The business selects policies and purchases directly through the platform. Payment via Paystack (card, bank transfer, USSD). Policy documents are stored in the platform. Renewal reminders are sent 30 days before expiry via WhatsApp. Sum insured adjustments are recommended annually based on naira depreciation and asset changes. Claims filing is guided through a step-by-step process with document checklist. Claims advocacy is provided throughout — InsureMatch does not disappear after the sale.

## 6. Claims Advocacy: The Long-Term Differentiator

Claims failure is the engine that powers the "insurance is a scam" narrative. Every denied claim, every 6-month delay, every agent who vanishes at claims time reinforces the belief that insurance does not work. This belief is the single biggest barrier to insurance penetration in Nigeria, and it is self-perpetuating: failed claims generate negative word-of-mouth, which prevents new purchases, which keeps penetration at 0.34%.

InsureMatch breaks this cycle by standing with the business at claims time, not just at sales time.

### Claims Advocacy Framework

**Pre-Claim (At Purchase):**
- Explain exactly what is and is not covered, in plain language, with Nigerian-specific examples
- Highlight exclusions explicitly: "This policy does NOT cover fires caused by generators. If you want that covered, select Policy B instead."
- Set realistic expectations on claims timelines and documentation requirements
- Store a pre-incident asset register (photos, valuations, receipts) so the business has documentation ready before a loss occurs

**During Claim:**
- Step-by-step claims filing guide with document checklist specific to each insurer and policy type
- Upload and organize claim documents through the platform
- Track claim status with timeline view — the business always knows where their claim stands
- Automated follow-up with insurer when claim milestones are missed
- Escalation to InsureMatch's advocacy team if the insurer delays beyond reasonable timelines

**Post-Claim:**
- Transparent publication of insurer claims payment statistics on the platform
- Insurer partner vetting: refuse to work with insurers known for claims avoidance
- Post-claim review: did the policy perform as expected? Adjust recommendations for renewal.
- Positive claims stories shared (with permission) to counter the "insurance is a scam" narrative

**Why This Matters Strategically:**
- Every successful claim InsureMatch facilitates becomes a testimonial that undoes years of negative word-of-mouth
- Claims advocacy creates extraordinary customer loyalty — the business will never leave a platform that fought for them
- Claims data becomes a proprietary asset: which insurers actually pay, which exclusions actually trigger, which documentation actually matters
- This data feeds back into the recommendation engine — InsureMatch can steer businesses toward insurers with proven claims performance

## 7. Value Chain Reimagination: Beyond Distribution

InsureMatch v1 reimagines insurance distribution. But distribution alone leaves the platform dependent on incumbent insurers for everything that matters — underwriting, policy issuance, claims adjudication. The long-term strategy builds proprietary infrastructure that creates independent value.

### Phase 1: Distribution Platform (Months 1-6)
Comparison engine and risk assessment. Dependent on insurer partners for quotes, policies, and claims. Value creation is in aggregation and education.

### Phase 2: Risk Intelligence Layer (Months 6-18)
Thousands of business risk profiles create a dataset that no incumbent insurer possesses. No one has ever systematically profiled Nigerian SME risk at this granularity — sector-specific loss frequencies, regional risk concentrations, asset valuation patterns, coverage gap distributions. This data becomes an independent asset with multiple monetization paths:
- Sell anonymized risk intelligence to insurers to improve their underwriting
- Use risk profiles to negotiate better rates from insurers (bulk risk data reduces underwriting uncertainty)
- Feed risk data into credit scoring models (a well-insured business is a lower credit risk)
- Offer risk consulting to businesses based on aggregate industry benchmarks ("companies like yours typically have X coverage")

### Phase 3: Claims Data Moat (Months 12-24)
Claims advocacy generates data on actual claims outcomes — which policies pay, which exclusions trigger, which documentation matters, which insurers are reliable. This is the most valuable data in insurance because it answers the question no one else can answer systematically: "If I buy this policy, will it actually pay when I need it?" This data:
- Feeds the recommendation engine with empirical performance data, not just policy terms
- Creates the most trusted insurance comparison in the market (scored on actual outcomes, not marketing)
- Becomes leverage in insurer negotiations: "Your claims payment rate is 62%. Leadway's is 84%. Fix it or we deprioritize your quotes."
- Attracts new insurer partners who want access to InsureMatch's distribution and are willing to compete on claims performance

### Phase 4: Embedded Insurance Infrastructure (Months 12-24)
APIs that enable any product in the suite (and eventually third-party platforms) to offer insurance at point of need:
- GenTrack registers a generator → automatic equipment insurance offer
- ClearFast books a shipment → automatic cargo/marine insurance offer
- PayMatch creates an invoice → automatic trade credit insurance offer
- Third-party e-commerce platforms → product liability insurance at point of sale

This is the Tolaram parallel: the noodle recipe was simple; the distribution infrastructure was the real innovation. InsureMatch's comparison engine is simple; the risk intelligence layer and embedded insurance infrastructure are the real value chain reimagination.

## 9. Key Screens & UX

### Design Principles

- **Trust forward:** Insurance is a trust product. Display insurer logos, NAICOM registration numbers, financial strength ratings, and claims payment statistics prominently. Every screen communicates legitimacy. For the downmarket persona, trust cues are even more important — show real testimonials from businesses in their region and sector.
- **Education integrated:** Do not assume the user knows what "business interruption insurance" means. Every insurance type has a one-line explainer and a "Learn more" link to a 2-minute case study featuring a Nigerian business. Use plain language: "covers your lost income while your workshop is being repaired" not "indemnifies against consequential loss arising from insured perils."
- **Naira-first, risk-aware:** All premiums and coverage amounts in NGN. USD equivalent in smaller text. Risk exposure quantified in naira terms the business owner understands: "A fire could cost you N250M in damage and N180M in lost revenue over 6 months."
- **WhatsApp native:** Share risk assessments, comparison results, quotes, and policy documents directly to WhatsApp. The MD will forward to their board/partners for approval. The workshop owner will share with their spouse.
- **"WhatsApp-Simple" interaction model:** Single-action screens. Green checkmark = done. Red = problem. Minimize clicks.
- **Numbers forward:** Lead with naira amounts. Big bold numbers at top. "N85M at risk" is the first thing the user sees after profile completion.

### Screen 1: Business Profile Wizard

A multi-step form presented as a guided conversation. Five steps for the secondary persona (manufacturers); three steps for the primary persona (workshop owners/traders):

**Full flow (5 steps):**
1. **Your Business:** Company name, CAC number (optional), sector (dropdown with 15 sectors), sub-sector, years in operation, annual revenue range (bands, not exact figures).
2. **Your Locations:** Primary location (state, LGA), additional locations, type of premises (owned factory, rented office, warehouse, retail shop).
3. **Your Assets:** Physical assets checklist with estimated values — buildings, machinery/equipment, vehicles, inventory/stock, IT equipment, furniture. Slider for "rough estimate" to reduce friction.
4. **Your People:** Number of employees, number of key executives (whose loss would significantly impact the business), largest customer concentration (% of revenue from top customer).
5. **Your Current Insurance:** Which types do you currently have? (Checkboxes.) For each: insurer name, policy number, annual premium, expiry date. "None" is the most common answer.

**Simplified flow (3 steps — for small businesses):**
1. **Your Business:** What do you do? (Select from visual icons: workshop, trading, food, transport, etc.) Where? (State/LGA). How long?
2. **Your Stuff:** Roughly how much is everything in your workshop/shop/warehouse worth? (Single slider: N1M to N50M.) Do you have a vehicle for deliveries? (Yes/No + estimated value.)
3. **Your Insurance:** Do you have any insurance right now? (Yes/No.) If yes, what type?

Each step has a progress indicator. Can be saved and resumed. Full flow takes 5-8 minutes. Simplified flow takes 2-3 minutes.

### Screen 2: Risk Assessment Dashboard (The Pull Trigger)

After profile completion, the user lands on their personalized risk assessment. This is the most important screen in the product — it creates the conversion moment.

**Top section: Risk Score** — a number out of 100 showing how well-protected the business is. Most businesses will score 5-20 out of 100 on first visit. The score is displayed as a large, bold number with color coding (red = 0-30, orange = 31-60, green = 61-100). Below the score: "You are X% uninsured. You have N[amount] in unprotected assets."

**The pull statement:** A single sentence that makes the risk concrete. Example: "If your workshop burned down tonight, you would lose N15M and have no way to recover. For N180K per year, you can protect everything." This is not a sales pitch — it is a fact, derived from the business's own data.

Below, a grid of insurance type cards. Each card shows:

### Insurer Integration Architecture

Most Nigerian insurers do not have APIs. The quote engine must handle three integration tiers:

**Tier 1 — API Integration (Target: 2-3 insurers)**
Direct REST API integration for real-time quote generation. InsureMatch sends business profile data, insurer API returns quote within seconds. Priority partners: Leadway, AXA Mansard, Custodian (most digitally advanced Nigerian insurers).

**Tier 2 — Email-to-API Bridge (Target: 5-8 insurers)**
InsureMatch generates a structured quote request email, sends to insurer's underwriting team, and parses the response email when it arrives. Turnaround: 2-24 hours. Automated email parsing extracts premium, coverage, and exclusions into the comparison table.

**Tier 3 — Manual Entry (Fallback)**
InsureMatch admin manually enters quotes received via phone or WhatsApp from insurers. Used during early days and for insurers who resist any digital integration.

## 13. Pricing & Unit Economics

InsureMatch charges zero to the business. Revenue comes entirely from insurer commissions. The free-to-business model is the only model that works for non-consumption conversion — any friction at the price layer eliminates the 99.66% who have never considered insurance.

| Revenue Stream | Rate | Basis |
|---------------|------|-------|
| **First-year commission** | 15-25% | Of annual premium paid by business. Standard industry rate for insurance intermediaries. Rate varies by insurance type (motor ~15%, fire ~20%, marine ~25%). |
| **Renewal commission** | 5-10% | Of annual premium on policy renewals. Lower than first-year but recurring. |
| **Portfolio management fee** | N50K/month (future) | For businesses with 5+ policies managed through InsureMatch. Optional premium service. |
| **Risk intelligence data** (Phase 2) | TBD | Anonymized risk profiling data sold to insurers for underwriting improvement. |

|--------|-------|-------|

## 16. Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Insurer Partnerships Take Too Long to Establish

**Likelihood:** High. Insurance companies are bureaucratic. Partnership agreements require legal review, commission negotiations, and technical integration. This could delay launch by months.

**Impact:** No insurers = no revenue. InsureMatch cannot generate commission income without insurer partners.

**Mitigations:**
- Launch risk assessment as a standalone product — generates value and pipeline before insurer partnerships close
- Start with one progressive insurer (Leadway or AXA Mansard) for a pilot. Do not wait for five
- Begin as a licensed insurance broker — this gives legal authority to intermediate and collect commissions without individual insurer partnerships
- Use Tier 3 (manual entry) integration initially — no API needed. Manually request and enter quotes via email/WhatsApp
- Offer insurers a zero-risk trial: "Give us 30 days. We send you 10 qualified leads. If none convert, no obligations."
- Leverage NAICOM's push for penetration — position InsureMatch as aligned with the regulator's agenda
- Use accumulated risk assessments as proof of demand: "We have 50 businesses waiting for quotes"

### Risk 2: Businesses Do Not Trust Online Insurance Purchase

**Likelihood:** High. Insurance already suffers from trust issues in Nigeria ("insurance is a scam"). Adding a digital intermediary adds another layer of distrust. This risk is amplified for the downmarket persona who has never interacted with any formal financial product beyond mobile money.

**Impact:** Businesses complete the risk assessment and view quotes but refuse to purchase through the platform, preferring to call the insurer directly (cutting InsureMatch out of the commission).

**Mitigations:**
- Display NAICOM registration prominently (InsureMatch must be registered as a broker or agent)
- Show insurer logos, registration numbers, and financial strength ratings on every quote
- Offer "call to complete" option — user views comparison online, then speaks with an InsureMatch advisor to finalize. Human touch for high-value decisions and first-time buyers
- Testimonials and case studies from early adopters, especially successful claims stories
- Claims advocacy as trust-building: "We do not just sell insurance — we fight for you when you need to claim"
- Track "platform bypass" rate — if users are viewing quotes and buying directly from insurers, consider switching to a lead-gen fee model instead of pure commission
- For the downmarket persona: use local language in communications, reference local businesses and incidents, have a phone number they can call

### Risk 3: Regulatory Complexity

**Likelihood:** Medium-High. Insurance distribution is regulated by NAICOM. Operating as an aggregator/broker requires licensing. Regulations may restrict how quotes are displayed or compared.

**Impact:** Legal action, fines, or forced shutdown if operating without proper licensing.

**Mitigations:**
- Obtain insurance broker license from NAICOM before launch (estimated 2-3 months, N500K-1M cost)
- Alternatively, partner with a licensed broker initially and operate under their license (revenue share)
- Engage insurance law firm for compliance review of platform design and comparison methodology
- Build relationship with NAICOM as a market development partner, not a disruptor — "we are expanding the market you want expanded"

### Risk 4: Low Average Premium Per Customer (Downmarket)

**Likelihood:** Medium-High. The downmarket persona (workshop owner, market trader) may only need one or two low-premium policies (e.g., N100-300K/year), making the commission per sale too small to sustain the business individually.

**Impact:** Revenue per downmarket customer is insufficient. Need hundreds of customers to reach meaningful revenue from this segment alone.

**Mitigations:**
- Downmarket customers are the volume play; midmarket customers are the revenue play. Both are needed.
- Digital delivery means near-zero marginal cost per additional downmarket customer (~N5K per policy sale)
- Insurance needs assessment drives coverage breadth — show the business all their gaps, not just the one they came for
- Downmarket volume builds the risk intelligence dataset, which has value independent of individual commissions
- Cross-sell from suite products brings midmarket customers with higher premiums
- At scale, N30-100K commission x 1000 downmarket policies = N30-100M annually

### Risk 5: Claims Experience Damages Reputation

**Likelihood:** Medium. When a business files a claim and the insurer delays or denies payment, the business blames InsureMatch — even though InsureMatch has no control over claims adjudication. This risk is existential for a non-consumption play: one bad claims story in a WhatsApp group can undo months of trust-building.

**Impact:** Negative word-of-mouth undermines the entire platform. Businesses tell their network "InsureMatch sold me useless insurance." The "insurance is a scam" narrative is reinforced rather than broken.

**Mitigations:**
- Claims advocacy is a core product feature, not an afterthought (see Section 6)
- Vet insurer partners on claims payment history before onboarding. Refuse to work with insurers known for claims avoidance
- Set clear expectations during purchase: explain exactly what is and is not covered, highlight exclusions explicitly
- Pre-incident asset documentation (photos, valuations) stored in platform to support claims
- Publish insurer claims payment statistics transparently on the platform
- Advocate on behalf of the business during claims disputes — this builds extraordinary loyalty
- Track and publish claims outcomes: "87% of claims filed through InsureMatch were paid within 30 days"
- Every successful claim is a story that breaks the "insurance is a scam" narrative — amplify these stories

### Risk 6: Commission Model Creates Cash Flow Gaps

**Likelihood:** Medium. Commissions are earned at point of sale and renewal (annually), not monthly. A month with no policy sales = zero revenue.

**Impact:** Unpredictable cash flow makes it difficult to plan and invest in growth.

**Mitigations:**
- Build a pipeline of businesses at various stages (assessment → quotes → comparison → decision) so policy sales are distributed over time
- Negotiate monthly commission payments from insurers rather than annual lump sums
